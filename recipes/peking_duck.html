Description

Peking duck is a Chinese dish that is traditionally made by roasting a whole duck to perfection and then slicing it into thin, crispy pieces. Here is a recipe for Peking duck that serves 4-6 people:

Ingredients

  • 1 whole duck, about 4-5 lbs
  • 2 tablespoons Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400Â°F.
  2. Rinse the duck and pat dry with paper towels. In a small bowl, mix together the Chinese five-spice powder, salt, soy sauce, honey, and sesame oil. Rub the mixture all over the outside and inside of the duck.
  3. Place the duck on a wire rack in a roasting pan. Add the water to the bottom of the pan. Roast the duck in the preheated oven for 1 hour and 45 minutes, or until the skin is crisp and golden brown.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the honey, hoisin sauce, soy sauce, and sesame oil to make the glaze. Brush the glaze over the duck and continue to roast for an additional 15-20 minutes or until the glaze is caramelized and the duck is cooked through.
  5. Remove the duck from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. Slice the duck into thin pieces and serve with the pancakes, scallions, cucumber, and hoisin sauce on the side. Enjoy!
